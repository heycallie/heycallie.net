april 4, 2026

MOVIE REVIEW

Interstellar (2014)
spoilers included! this, as of now, has just become one of my favorite movies of all time. i generally love a good science fiction, especially one with great worldbuilding -- which interstellar most definitely has. the initial setting of an earth, not far into the future (or possibly in the past) where food is limited due to humanity's decisions (something that could become VERY real today) scratches an itch i really didn't know i had. not to mention, the ending with the black hole becoming a catalyst to navigating the fifth dimension (space & time) leading back to many foreshadowed and unexplained events throughout the movie is something i genuinely adore so much, especially in science fiction. Nolan also managed to incorporate alien events whilst, by the end of the movie, the only living beings throughout are humans. bending through time and creating these sorts of paradoxes are probably my favorite trope ever in sci-fi, and this movie really did not disappoint. though, i can't gloss over possibly the most talked about aspect of the entire movie; the visuals. The Black Hole: fucking a-MAZING. I've obviously seen sneak peeks of it over my time on the internet (unavoidable, really) but it still blew my mind away seeing it in the context of the entire movie. the subtle details all throughout the movie as well really caught my eye as well, one example being, when the main character descends into the black hole, he gets smaller to a point until appearing to be frozen, something that is scientifically accurate to objects crossing the event horizon in real life (nerrrdd here...) :D genuinely, i have so much more to say about this movie, but i shouldn't elongate this too much. 10/10, would absolutely watch again. wish i was there to see it in theatres.
